02 / The cost of moving too fast

Most product decisions get made before the right questions are honestly answered.

Whether you're shaping a new concept, refining an existing SKU, or planning a relaunch — the strategic conversation that should happen first usually happens after the spend is committed.

P · 01

Weak positioning

A clear product, an unclear reason to choose it. The shelf is unforgiving when the message isn't.

P · 02

Wrong format

The right active in the wrong vessel. Format dictates ritual, repeat purchase and unit economics.

P · 03

Ingredient complexity

Stacked actives that read as a science fair. Credibility is lost long before the first sip.

P · 04

Claim confusion

Functional language that overpromises, underdelivers, or wanders into regulatory grey zones.

P · 05

Crowded category

Entering a battlefield without a defensible angle, a real wedge, or a buyer who is actively switching.

P · 06

Capital before validation

A launch path that demands more cash, channels and complexity than the concept can support.

12 / Common questions

Before you buy, honest answers.

Is this just ChatGPT with a nice template?+

No. Gravity runs on the Aterra Intelligence OS — a structured diagnostic system built on real CPG launch frameworks developed across a decade of product development, retail placement, and go-to-market execution in the natural channel. The output is a scored, multi-dimensional brief with a written recommendation — not a generated essay. On the $1,500 path, it is reviewed and signed by Gustavo Nader personally.

Is my concept kept confidential?+

Yes. Your submission is used solely to generate your brief. It is not shared, stored for model training, or accessible to third parties. If you are working on something sensitive, the Aterra Reviewed Brief includes a confidentiality commitment.

What if my concept is very early — just an idea?+

That is the best time to use it. The brief is designed for concepts at any stage: raw idea, formulated product, existing SKU, or repositioning. The earlier you run the diagnostic, the more capital it protects.

How is this different from Mintel, SPINS, or NielsenIQ?+

Those platforms give you data. Gravity gives you a decision. The brief synthesizes category signals, consumer language, ingredient complexity, competitive positioning, and execution risk into one written recommendation: build, refine, reposition, validate first, or pause. Analyst-grade output at a founder-accessible price.

What do I need to submit?+

Plain language. Your concept or product, target consumer, format, key ingredients or claims, and your current situation. No deck, no spreadsheet, no formal document. The intake takes about 15 minutes.
